Cybersecurity Rules and Best Practices Policy

1. Password Management
All employees must follow strict password management practices:
- Passwords must be at least 12 characters long and include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days.
- Password reuse is prohibited for the last 12 passwords.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) must be enabled for all corporate accounts.
- Never share passwords with anyone, including IT support staff.
- Use a company-approved password manager for storing credentials.

2. Email Security
Email is a common attack vector for cybercriminals:
- Do not open attachments from unknown senders.
- Verify the sender's email address before clicking any links.
- Report suspicious emails to the IT security team immediately.
- Never send sensitive data (passwords, financial information, personal data) via unencrypted email.
- Be cautious of phishing attempts that create a sense of urgency.
- Use email encryption for sensitive communications.

3. Device Security
All company devices must be properly secured:
- Enable full disk encryption on all laptops and mobile devices.
- Install and maintain up-to-date antivirus software.
- Enable automatic operating system and software updates.
- Lock your computer when stepping away from your desk (Windows+L or Cmd+Control+Q).
- Do not connect personal USB drives or external devices to company computers.
- Report lost or stolen devices to IT security within 1 hour.

4. Network Security
Follow these rules when connecting to networks:
- Only connect to approved corporate Wi-Fi networks.
- Use VPN when working remotely or using public Wi-Fi.
- Do not attempt to bypass network security controls or firewalls.
- Report any unusual network activity or performance issues.
- Do not install unauthorized network devices (routers, switches, access points).

5. Data Classification and Handling
All company data must be classified and handled appropriately:
- Public: Information that can be freely shared.
- Internal: Information for internal use only, not to be shared externally.
- Confidential: Sensitive information requiring restricted access.
- Restricted: Highly sensitive information with strict access controls.
- Always apply the appropriate classification label to documents and emails.
- Dispose of physical documents containing sensitive information using cross-cut shredders.

6. Incident Response
In case of a security incident:
- Immediately report the incident to the IT Security team via the security hotline.
- Do not attempt to investigate or remediate the incident on your own.
- Preserve evidence by not deleting files, emails, or logs related to the incident.
- Document what happened, when it happened, and what actions were taken.
- Cooperate fully with the incident response team during investigation.

7. Social Engineering Awareness
Be aware of social engineering tactics:
- Never provide sensitive information over the phone without verifying the caller's identity.
- Be suspicious of unsolicited requests for information, even from people claiming to be colleagues.
- Verify unusual requests through a separate communication channel.
- Tailgating: Do not allow unauthorized individuals to follow you into secure areas.
