Employee Code of Conduct Policy

1. Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct:
- Treat all colleagues, clients, and stakeholders with respect and dignity.
- Maintain a positive and collaborative work environment.
- Communicate professionally in all forms of communication (email, chat, meetings, phone).
- Be punctual for meetings and work commitments.
- Dress appropriately according to the company dress code policy.
- Refrain from using offensive language or engaging in inappropriate behavior.

2. Workplace Harassment and Discrimination
The company has zero tolerance for harassment and discrimination:
- Harassment includes verbal, physical, visual, or sexual harassment.
- Discrimination based on race, gender, age, religion, disability, sexual orientation, or any other protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.
- All complaints of harassment or discrimination will be investigated promptly and confidentially.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment or discrimination is prohibited.
- Employees must complete annual anti-harassment training.
- Report any incidents to HR or use the anonymous reporting hotline.

3. Conflict of Interest
Employees must avoid situations that create conflicts of interest:
- Disclose any personal or financial interests that may conflict with company interests.
- Do not engage in outside employment or business activities that conflict with your role.
- Do not accept gifts, entertainment, or favors that could influence business decisions.
- Gifts exceeding $50 in value must be reported to your manager and the compliance team.
- Do not use company resources for personal gain.

4. Confidentiality and Intellectual Property
Employees must protect company information and intellectual property:
- Do not disclose confidential business information to unauthorized individuals.
- Sign and adhere to the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA).
- All work products created during employment belong to the company.
- Return all company materials, documents, and equipment upon termination.
- Do not use proprietary information from previous employers.

5. Use of Company Resources
Company resources should be used responsibly:
- Company equipment, software, and facilities are for business use primarily.
- Limited personal use of company resources is permitted but should not interfere with work.
- Do not install unauthorized software on company devices.
- Internet usage should comply with the company's acceptable use policy.
- Do not use company email for personal business or unauthorized purposes.

6. Attendance and Work Hours
Employees must adhere to attendance policies:
- Maintain regular attendance and punctuality.
- Notify your supervisor in advance of any planned absences.
- Follow the company's procedures for reporting sick days or personal leave.
- Remote work must be approved by your manager and follow the remote work policy.
- Overtime must be pre-approved by your supervisor.

7. Reporting Violations
All employees have a responsibility to report violations:
- Report any violations of this code of conduct to your manager, HR, or the compliance team.
- Use the anonymous ethics hotline for sensitive reports.
- The company will protect whistleblowers from retaliation.
- Failure to report known violations may result in disciplinary action.
- All reports will be investigated thoroughly and confidentially.

8. Disciplinary Actions
Violations of the code of conduct may result in disciplinary action:
- Verbal warning for minor first-time offenses.
- Written warning for repeated minor offenses or moderate violations.
- Suspension for serious violations.
- Termination for severe violations, including fraud, theft, violence, or repeated misconduct.
- Legal action may be taken in cases involving criminal activity.
